In 1998, I helped build The Electric Newspaper — Australia's first independent online daily newspaper, published out of Adelaide by Virtual Artists and edited by Hendrik Gout. For seven months it covered SA politics (the ETSA privatisation, the Ingerson scandal), the Adelaide Festival and Fringe, and many other topics, with 711 articles from working journalists, academics, and politicians.

The site went dark in July 1998 and sat as an archive for over 25 years. This year I restored it: remapped thousands of broken assets, fixed navigation, and rebuilt it, for that true 1998 experience, inside a simulated Netscape Navigator 4 browser at 800×600. There is also a searchable index. The original article text is untouched.

It's now live, browsable, and being prepared for submission to the National Library of Australia's PANDORA/Trove archive.

Have a look: https://7w4tgv5gs5-commits.github.io/electric-newspaper/
