**Site:** The Electric Newspaper
**Live URL:** https://7w4tgv5gs5-commits.github.io/electric-newspaper/

The Electric Newspaper was Australia's first independent online daily newspaper, published from Adelaide, South Australia, between February and July 1998. It was produced by the web design studio Virtual Artists and edited by journalist Hendrik Gout, publishing 711 articles across eleven sections — news, essays, arts, film, books, sport, food, travel, fringe, letters, and a regular column.

Its seven-month run coincided with several significant South Australian events: the Olsen Liberal government's privatisation of ETSA, the parliamentary privileges scandal involving Deputy Premier Graham Ingerson, the 1998 Adelaide Festival and Fringe, and the Adelaide Rams' final NRL season. The paper closed in July 1998 and has had no online presence since.

**Restoration:** The original site was preserved as a tar file from its 1998 Unix host and sat dormant until 2026, when I restored it — recovering broken file references (the original archive process had truncated all filenames to 8 characters, breaking ~18,000 image links across all 711 articles), rebuilding navigation, and republishing it as a static site. The 1998 article text and HTML content is unmodified; only image paths, navigation links, and one legacy script were corrected for modern browsers, with the originals preserved in code comments.

I believe this fits your selection guidelines as an Australian, online-only publication with no print equivalent, of historical and local research value documenting South Australian media, politics, and culture in 1998.

**Copyright note:** The original 1998 article content remains under its original copyright; my restoration work (path fixes, navigation, and accompanying contextual pages) is released under CC0. I'm happy to discuss rights and access arrangements for the original content as needed.
